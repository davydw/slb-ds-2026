# Wagon DS Bootcamp 2025 - Q2

## :star2:  More AI in DS :star2:

We are bringing more AI into the Data Science & AI bootcamp :rocket:, something we all have been longing for. 
It’s quite a big change, especially in the flow of the first three weeks because we are making some room to add new days in the Deep Learning module.

:dart: The two Q2 online batches are running this beta program. The idea is that based on the feedbacks from both teachers and students, we’ll adapt and roll out in Q3.

:check: Except for the two new AI days in the Deep Learning module, all the content has already been updated on Kitt.

:page_facing_up:  Below you will find a high level overview of all the changes we are bringing into this program. When you prepare for a day, also check if there are **Teacher Guidelines** (an extra tab on the day’s lecture page on Kitt). New and heavily modified days feature these.

**One advice**: prepare well ahead, read these guidelines, and check the teacher guidelines. Also make sure to know where the day fits within the curriculum, and think of what students have already seen, and what not.

**Questions? Noticed a bug? Suggestions for improvement?** Reach out directly to ***Jules Van Rie*** for this beta program. No need to file issues through the teachers repo.


## :star2:  New AI days

* **RNN** :brain: and **NLP** :speaking_head_in_silhouette: are split in two days again
* We then proceed with a slightly updated Transformers day
* A full day on **RAG and agents** :books: (replacing the mini-lecture during projects
* A new day on **Generative AI with images** :female-artist::skin-tone-2: and **Reinforcement Learning** :chess_pawn:

More details below.

## :star:  Other new days

* A **Dashboarding** :chart_with_upwards_trend: day with plotly and dash replacing both Data Viz and Olist Communicate. No more nitty gritty stuff trying to make a beautiful matplotlib chart, and no more notebook based presentations and frustrations.
* A **Coding Properly :mechanical_arm: and Reboot** :repeat: day at the end of week 1 to reinforce coding skills (OOP, TDD, documentation, git, …) and to recap all of the important concepts of the first week.

## :arrows_clockwise:  Other changes

To do this we have to **free up some space**, and rearrange the schedule the first weeks a bit

* On day 2, :python:  Python, we moved the OOP challenge to day 5 and bring in a new challenge on using functions. We also changed the order of the challenges.
* On day 3, we move immediately from :python: Python into :numpy: **NumPy and :pandas:Pandas** basics.
* On day 4, we’ll go into **Data Sourcing with Pandas**, integrating the essentials from the removed Python Data Sourcing day.
* Day 5 is the new **Coding Properly and Reboot** day.
* We kick off the second week with a fresh head to go into **Statistics** (no more Friday evening recap with the CLT when everyone is exhausted :face_exhaling:).
* **Algebra and Calculus** moves further down the schedule as a warmup for Machine Learning.
* **SQL Basics** goes to Python week 0 in an online environment. SQL Advanced goes right in front of Decision Science to reinforce the concepts about relationships and joins. In week 0 we removed Advanced Classes.
* **Decision Science** is reduced to four days: OOP concepts have already been covered in Coding Properly. We simplify the way we set up Olist and get rid of the file handling headache, which is what most students spend the first day on. We also simplify the CEO request challenge, and reserve the last day for data visualization and dashboarding.
* In project weeks we replace the Gen AI lecture (which becomes a day in its own) by an introduction to **Agile** project management. We will do this on the second day of projects.


All of this allows us to start **Machine Learning** already **in the third week of the bootcamp**, and to introduce three extra Deep Learning day :partying_face:. And at the same time we bring in more useful concepts for students.


## Outline for the new days

### :transformer: Transformers and LLM

Building on the existing day:

* In the lecture:
    * Focus a bit more on encoder / decoder / encoder-decoders
    * Go briefly into metrics for generative language models
    * Fine tuning and prompting
    * Generation parameters (temperature, top-k, top-p)
* Challenges:
    * Move Tensor Trials to the back (in front of Your Own GPT)
    * New challenge on working with Generative LLMs. Prompting an SLM. Prompting a Generative model for classification. Working with generation parameters.
    * The LangChain challenge moves to the next day
* Recap: recap concepts + intro Vision Transformers

### :books: RAG and Agents

* Lecture:
    * Why RAG? What is it?
    * Document embedding, similarity search, vector dbs
    * Tool/ function calling
    * Agents
    * Multi-agents
    * Optimisation methods for LLMs (quantization, distillation, ...)
* Challenges:
    * Tool calling
    * Intro to LangChain (from Transformers day)
    * Simple Agent
    * Advanced RAG
    * Multi-agent
* Recap: recap concepts / challenge correction / multi-modal models

### :chess_pawn: Reinforcement Learning and Gen AI for images :female-artist::skin-tone-2:

* Lecture RL:
    * Intro to RL
    * RL components
    * RL algorithms
    * Libraries (gymnasium and Stable Baselines)
* Lecture Gen AI for images:
    * From autoencoder to VAE
    * GAN
    * Diffusion models
* Challenges:
    * Your First RL
    * Q learning (applied)
    * Deep Q learning (applied)
    * Create your own gym environment
    * GAN: number generator
* Recap: Overview of DL + when to chose what
